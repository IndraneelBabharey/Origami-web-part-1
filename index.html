Intresting facts about Camel

  • Newborn camels don't have humps. They gradually grow as they get older.
  • Camels are smarter than horses.
  • Camels can live around 40 - 50 years.

Intresting facts about Panda

  • Pandas eat almost nothing but bamboo shoots and leaves.
  • Pandas can climb as high as 13,000 feet and are also very good swimmers.
  • An adult female panda weighs 200 pounds.

Intresting facts about Chameleon

  • There are around 160 species of chameleon.
  • The panther chameleon changes its color to yellow or red when it’s angry.
  • When a chameleon is cold, it will become darker in color. Dark colors attract heat better than light ones.

Intresting facts about Teddy Bear

  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
  • The official term for a teddy bear collector is an ‘arctophile’.

Intresting facts about flying Cicada

  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.
  • The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.
  • Cicadas can turn themselves into an evaporative cooler.

Intresting facts about Pigeon

  • Pigeons have been present with humans since ages
  • They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to, and can thus hear distant storms and volcanoes.
  • Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph. The fastest recorded speed is 92.5 mph.
